About the Company

Square Enix, a prominent figure in the video game industry, emerged from the amalgamation of two iconic Japanese companies: Square Co., Ltd. and Enix Corporation. Square Co., Ltd., founded by Masafumi Miyamoto in 1986, gained international acclaim with the release of "Final Fantasy" in 1987. This groundbreaking role-playing game (RPG) series solidified Square's reputation as a leading developer in the gaming world. Throughout its history, Square produced a plethora of successful titles, including the beloved "Chrono" series, "Secret of Mana," and "SaGa" franchise.

On the other hand, Enix Corporation, established by Yasuhiro Fukushima in 1975, initially focused on publishing tabloid-style newspapers and computer games. However, it rose to prominence with the release of "Dragon Quest" (known as "Dragon Warrior" in North America) in 1986. This RPG series rivaled "Final Fantasy" in popularity and laid the foundation for Enix's success in the gaming industry.

In 2003, Square Co., Ltd. and Enix Corporation merged, forming Square Enix Holdings Co., Ltd. This strategic move aimed to strengthen both companies' positions in the competitive gaming market and leverage their respective strengths. The merger brought together Square's expertise in storytelling and graphics with Enix's mastery in game design and publishing.

Post-merger, Square Enix continued its legacy of producing and publishing groundbreaking games. It expanded beyond traditional RPGs, exploring new genres like action-adventure, sports, and massively multiplayer online (MMO) games. Notable franchises born from this era include "Kingdom Hearts" and "NieR."

Furthermore, Square Enix diversified its portfolio by venturing into film production and merchandise licensing, demonstrating its commitment to holistic entertainment experiences. In recent years, the company has embraced mobile gaming, releasing titles for smartphones and tablets.

Despite changes in the gaming landscape, Square Enix remains a titan in the industry, known for its captivating storytelling, innovative gameplay mechanics, and stunning visuals. With a rich history spanning decades, Square Enix continues to captivate audiences worldwide with its unforgettable gaming experiences.

The creations of the King
  • Final Fantasy (1987)

    • A legendary RPG series known for its epic storytelling, memorable characters, and innovative gameplay mechanics.

  • Mana (formerly known as Seiken Densetsu) (1991)

    • An action RPG series featuring vibrant worlds, real-time combat, and a focus on cooperative multiplayer gameplay.

  • SaGa (1989)

    • A series of non-linear RPGs known for their open-ended gameplay, unique character progression systems, and branching storylines.

  • Front Mission (1995)

    • A tactical RPG series set in a futuristic world, featuring mechs, deep customization options, and strategic turn-based combat.

  • Chrono (1995)

    • An acclaimed series of time-traveling RPGs known for their complex narratives, multiple endings, and innovative gameplay mechanics.

  • Parasite Eve (1998)

    • A horror RPG series blending elements of science fiction, survival horror, and RPG gameplay, set in a modern-day urban environment.

  • Kingdom Hearts (2002)

    • A crossover action RPG series featuring characters from Disney and Square Enix franchises, known for its compelling story and real-time combat.

  • The World Ends with You (2007)

    • An action RPG set in modern-day Tokyo, featuring unique touchscreen controls, stylish visuals, and a compelling storyline.

  • Dragon Quest (formerly known as Dragon Warrior) (1986)

    • A classic RPG series known for its traditional gameplay, charming characters, and whimsical world design.

  • Bravely (2012)

    • A modern take on classic turn-based RPGs, featuring a unique job system, strategic combat, and charming art style.

  • Nier (2010)

    • An action RPG series known for its thought-provoking storytelling, philosophical themes, and multiple narrative perspectives.

  • Octopath Traveler (2018)

    • An RPG featuring eight unique characters with their own stories, set in a visually stunning world inspired by retro RPGs.
